Our connections.

Curated by Medhasri Veldurthi

The three artworks I have chosen primarily come together based on the idea of connectedness. We as humans see ourselves as starkly different from one another, yet we are all one in the same and I see that each of these pieces reflects that sentiment in a unique way. The first piece I have chosen, STARRYNIGHT is the very definition of connection. The element of human interaction brings together how we certain things as “brighter” or “connected” but those are all just an illusion we conjure ourselves. At the end of the day, each of these archived emails is simply a star in which we denote meaning, much in the way that we view ourselves. Therefore, the connections we make amongst ourselves are also an illusion as we are all one in the same, The next piece, Apartments, is simple yet conveys this image clearly. I feel as though this brings together the idea that people each have their own sense of home yet the simple nature of the activities described show all of us have the most primal of associations for each of these spaces. Finally, the piece In:verse is slightly different from the other two in the sense that it emphasizes the user's interactions. Once again, the user can connect and themselves sees how despite each of the words having a single meaning, put together they can generate unique pieces that strike the eye. Once again, this is a reflection of how we view ourselves and attach meaning to our connections to generate a greater picture than the static representation itself. Overall, I enjoy how each of these pieces manages to foster a sense of community.


Apartments, Martin Wattenberg, 2001

Depicting a simple floor plan it has words written over writing the most basic of activities in each room of the home.


STARRYNIGHT by ALEX GALLOWAY, MARK TRIBE, AND MARTIN WATTENBERG 1999

The idea of this piece is an archive of emails as stars, where each star becomes brighter the more it is visited and constellations are formed amongst the keywords.


In:verse by Sukanya Aneja, 2020


The pieces base a linear meaning behind each of the words the user types to generate art through programming.
